## About Me

I am an Assistant Professor of AI and Robotics at Al-Zaytoonah University of Jordan, where I am dedicated to shaping the next generation of engineers and scientists. My academic journey is supported by a PhD in AI and Robotics and enriched by industry experience as a research scientist for a leading medical devices company. This dual background provides me with a unique perspective that bridges theoretical knowledge with practical, real-world applications.

My research is concentrated at the intersection of neuroscience, robotics, and artificial intelligence, with a primary focus on **neural-computer interfaces (NCI)**. I am particularly passionate about developing advanced control systems for prosthetic limbs using electromyography (EMG) signals, exploring the potential of EMG-based control of prosthetic arms to restore intuitive and natural motor function. Furthermore, my work extends to neuromodulation, investigating how targeted electrical or magnetic stimulation can enhance neural plasticity and motor learning.

## Teaching Philosophy

In my teaching, I champion a philosophy of pragmatic mastery through two core principles:

1. **Project-Based Learning (PBL):** True understanding comes from doing. My courses are structured around hands-on projects that challenge students to build tangible solutions, synthesizing knowledge over time.
2. **The 80/20 Principle:** I design my curriculum to identify and focus on the critical 20% of foundational knowledge that unlocks 80% of the practical skills needed to excel in the field.
